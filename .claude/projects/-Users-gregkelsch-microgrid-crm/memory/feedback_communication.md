name: Communication preferences
description: How Greg prefers to work — direct questions, no multi-choice walls, self-review before shipping
type: feedback
---

Keep questions simple and one at a time. Don't present walls of multi-choice options — Greg said "what are you asking me?" when I did that. Just ask plainly.

Self-review implementations before presenting. Catch issues like invented disposition values or incomplete cascades before shipping, not after.

Greg tests immediately and gives feedback with screenshots. He's decisive and doesn't need hand-holding on decisions — just present the tradeoff clearly.

**Why:** Multi-choice question blocks slow him down and feel like bureaucracy. He wants fast iteration with quality.

**How to apply:** Ask one simple question at a time. When unsure, state what you'd recommend and do it unless he says otherwise. Review your own code critically before committing.
